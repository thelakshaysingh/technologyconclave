11-12 July 2026 · SOA University, Bhubaneswar

Startup Display Guidelines

Showcase your innovative products, processes, and engineering solutions to industry leaders, researchers, and policymakers.

Overview

Guidelines & Expectations

The Indian National Academy of Engineering (INAE) invites technology entrepreneurs and startups to showcase their innovative products, processes, and engineering solutions during the INAE Technology Conclave 2026. The event offers an excellent platform for interaction with industry leaders, researchers, investors, policymakers, and eminent scientists.

Exhibitor Expectations

Selected startups are expected to adhere to the following operational and presentation requirements:

  • Booth Presence: At least one founder or technical representative must be present at the stall during all designated display sessions.
  • Setup Timeline: All physical and digital kiosk setups should be completed on 10 July 2026 between 14:00 and 20:00 hrs.
  • Product Demonstration: Startups are encouraged to provide live demonstrations, working prototypes, or interactive digital simulations instead of static displays.
  • Prototype Limits: Any physical prototype should be transportable through standard Light Commercial Vehicles (LCVs).
  • Pitch Readiness: Teams should be prepared with a concise 3-minute elevator pitch for delegates, jury members, and visitors.
  • Marketing Material: Participants should bring their own banners, brochures, and digital presentation material in USB format.
  • Safety Compliance: Any hazardous material or high-voltage equipment must be declared to the organizers at least 15 days in advance. All branding material should maintain professional and academic decorum suitable for a national technology conclave.

Startups are encouraged to prominently display:

  • Company Name & Logo
  • Website / QR Code / Contact Information
  • Product USP and Application Areas

Facilities Provided

SOA University will provide the following standard infrastructural support to all selected participants:

  • Standard Stall Space: One pre-fabricated octanorm booth measuring 3m × 3m.
  • Basic Furniture: One draped presentation table, two chairs, and one dustbin per stall.
  • Power Supply: One 5A electrical plug point. Additional power requirements may be requested in advance.
  • Connectivity: Wi-Fi access for up to two devices per startup. Startups requiring high-bandwidth internet, dedicated LAN, cloud access, or special software support must inform organizers in advance. Participants are advised to maintain offline backups of presentations, videos, and demo applications. Organizers shall not be responsible for disruptions caused by third-party internet outages or software incompatibility.
  • Lighting: Two spotlights within the stall area.
  • Security & Maintenance: General venue security, overnight hall locking, and regular cleaning services.
  • Hospitality: Complimentary lunch and refreshments for up to two registered team members per day.

Registration & Confirmation

  • Only shortlisted startups completing registration formalities within the stipulated timeline shall be allotted stalls.
  • Stall allocation will be done by the organizing committee and may be categorized sector-wise or theme-wise.
  • Stall positions once allotted shall not be exchanged or modified without prior approval from the organizers.

Display Timings

The Startup Display sessions shall be ongoing during the INAE Technology Conclave 2026 from morning 9.30 am to 6 pm for both 11-12 July 2026, however, these shall be evaluated as per the following schedule:

  • 11 July 2026 (Saturday): Startup Display during Parallel Session from 16:45 hrs. to 17:45 hrs.
  • 12 July 2026 (Sunday): Startup Display during Parallel Session from 11:45 hrs. to 12:45 hrs.
  • 12 July 2026 (Sunday): Startup Display during Parallel Session from 14:30 hrs. to 15:30 hrs.

Preparation

Application & Evaluation Information Sheet

Applicants for the Startup Display are requested to provide the following details in a concise and structured format. The information provided shall be used by the Screening Committee and Jury for evaluation and shortlisting.

A. Basic Startup Information

  • • Name of the Startup
  • • Year of Incorporation / Inception
  • • Type of Organization
  • • CIN / DPIIT / Startup India Number
  • • Registered Office Address
  • • Website / Social Media Links
  • • Contact Details of Founder / Rep.
  • • Number of Team Members / Employees
  • • Sector/ Domain of operation
  • • Current stage of Startup Idea (Idea/Prototype/ MVP/ Early Revenue/ Growth Stage)

B. Founder & Institutional Details

  • • Names and Background of Founders
  • • Academic and Professional Expertise
  • • Affiliated Incubator / Accelerator / Lab
  • • Details of Mentors / Technical Advisors
  • • Collaboration with Industry / Govt.

C. Technology & Innovation

  • • Title / Name of Product or Service
  • • Brief Abstract (Max 300 words)
  • • Problem / Societal Need addressed
  • • Novelty and Uniqueness
  • • Engineering Components involved
  • • Current Stage of Development
  • • Technology Readiness Level (TRL)
  • • Intellectual Property Generated
  • • Competitive Advantage

D. Business & Financial

  • • Target Market and End Users
  • • Business / Revenue Model
  • • Approximate Costing / Pricing Model
  • • Current customers/ Pilot Deployments
  • • Funding Received /Raised (Grant/ Angel/ Vc/ Institutional/ Bootsrapped)
  • • Annual Turnover / Revenue Generated
  • • Major Achievements (Awards or Recognitions, Patents)

E. Demonstration Needs

  • • Nature of Demonstration Proposed
  • • Space / Power / Internet or Special Infrastructure Requirements
  • • Safety or Compliance Considerations
  • • Audio-Visual Equipment Required
  • • Whether Live Demonstration, Prototype, Model or Digital Display will be used

F. Additional Information

  • • Expected Societal/Industrial Impact
  • • Scalability and Commercialization
  • • Future Development Roadmap
  • • Any Other Relevant Information

Indicative Evaluation Parameters for Jury

(May be asked for proof during the Conclave)

Innovation & Originality Engineering & Technical Depth Practical Utility Scalability & Market Potential Commercial Viability Societal & Environmental Impact Quality of Presentation Industry Adoption Potential
