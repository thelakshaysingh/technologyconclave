Nestled in Odisha, Bhubaneswar — the Temple City of India — blends over 2,000 years of Kalinga heritage with a thriving Smart City and premier academic institutions. Here's a glimpse of what awaits you.

Bhubaneswar Old Town

Lingaraj Temple

One of the oldest and grandest temples in India, dedicated to Lord Shiva and dating to the 11th century — the crowning jewel of Kalinga architecture, soaring above a sacred enclosure of 150 smaller shrines.

Old Town, Bhubaneswar

The Temple City

Bhubaneswar once had over 7,000 temples. Today it preserves a remarkable living heritage — Mukteswar, Rajarani, Ananta Vasudeva and dozens more — spanning the 7th to 13th centuries.

Dhauli, Bhubaneswar

Dhauli Shanti Stupa

On this hill in 261 BCE, Emperor Ashoka witnessed the devastation of the Kalinga War and turned to Buddhism. The gleaming Indo-Japanese Peace Pagoda stands as a symbol of harmony, with panoramic views over the plains.

Konark, Odisha · 65 km from venue

Konark Sun Temple

A UNESCO World Heritage Site built in the 13th century in the form of a colossal stone chariot dedicated to the Sun God — one of the finest examples of medieval Indian craftsmanship.

Konark Sun Temple · East Entrance

The Golden Triangle

Bhubaneswar, Puri and Konark form Odisha's celebrated "Golden Triangle" — landmarks within 2 hours of each other, drawing visitors for heritage, beaches and the world-famous Rath Yatra festival.

Konark Sun Temple · Chariot Wheel

Stone Sundials of Konark

The 24 intricately carved stone wheels of the Sun Temple are functional sundials — each spoke marks an interval of time, demonstrating the precision and vision of ancient Odishan craftsmen.

Heritage & Temples

Over 700 temples spanning the 7th–13th centuries dot the Old Town. Lingaraj, Mukteswar, Rajarani and Parasurameswar are all within a short drive of the venue.

Knowledge Hub

Home to IIT Bhubaneswar, AIIMS, NISER, CSIR-IMMT, ICT-IOC and SOA University — one of India's fastest-growing centres for education, research and technology.

Day Trips

Konark Sun Temple (UNESCO) and Puri's beachfront are 1.5–2 hours away. The Odisha State Museum and Tribal Art Museum are excellent half-day options within the city.

A short note on Bhubaneswar - the Temple City and Smart City capital of Odisha - and how to make the most of your time outside the conclave hall.

Heritage

Bhubaneswar's Old Town is dotted with sandstone temples spanning the 7th to 13th centuries - Lingaraj, Mukteswar, Rajarani and Parasurameswar are within a short drive of the venue.

Cuisine

Try Odia staples like dalma, chenna poda and macha besara at local restaurants in Kalinganagar and Saheed Nagar. Conclave dinners feature regional specialities.

Side Trips

Konark Sun Temple (UNESCO World Heritage) and Puri's beachfront are within 1.5-2 hours by road. The State Museum and Tribal Art Museum offer concentrated half-day visits.
